Objective of an Appraisal

The aim of an evaluation is to continually enhance the standard of learning, skills, and procedures within the organisation.



It is to provide a routine opportunity (formalised by annual discussion) for a thorough performance analysis.



It offers support and motivation for satisfactory or excellent results for those being evaluated, as well as support and advice for those needing improvement.

It assists in developing career opportunities if so desired.



Process

Prior to the set day and time of review, both the employee and the manager obtain and complete the Appraisal Form.



The individual being assessed completes the ratings under the 'Employee' heading and ensures that the rating is substantiated with as many comments as are appropriate.

The reviewer is going to do the same.



During the discussion, the Reviewer will enter the ratings from the person being appraised into the Reviewer’s copy. Please note that there is always an opportunity for either to change their ratings because of the discussion.



A comment must be provided for a rating that is ‘does not meet’ to ensure that action points are developed forming part of the Personal Development Plan (PDP).

Comments for ratings o ‘Meet’ and ‘Exceed’ are at the discretion of the Reviewer but highly encouraged.



Objectives for next period

Both parties should decide the priorities for the coming year.

They should be aligned with the company's focus areas, should be measurable, realistic and should have time frames set for analysis and completion.



Development Needs

Both parties should assess development needs.

An action plan should be drawn up to meet these development needs, and follow-up dates should be identified for review.

The employee is responsible for driving and tracking their growth strategy.

Support must be offered and provided by the reviewer.
