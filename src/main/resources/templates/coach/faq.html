Frequently Asked Questions

What is an Annual Performance Review?

  • An Annual Performance Review involves a formal discussion about your development and performance.
  • The review is a planning process.
  • It involves setting a plan of action for the next period and reviewing what has been achieved in the last period.


Why do I need one?

  • Regular Performance Reviews help create a corporate culture that promotes personal and business success.
  • It provides an opportunity for both of you, you, and your line manager, to discuss openly your strengths and weaknesses, and how we can assist in both.


Who gets one?

  • Everyone in the business should have the opportunity to have a formal chat.


What if I am nervous about the meeting?

  • Anything that is new can always be unsettling, but rest assured, the leaders of the business have been trained in how to conduct constructive Performance Reviews. Nothing should be a surprise!
  • This is not a session for pointing out what is wrong – it is the opportunity to highlight what you have done well, and where you require assistance.


What do I do once I have been told the date for my review?

  • Record notes on this document prior to your meeting.
  • Think objectively – have I really done this well or could I have done a bit more of this in the last 12 months.
  • Have a comment against each of the headings supporting your rating.


What if I can’t think of specific examples?

  • That is okay. You do not have to have specific examples for each section – but try to have as much information as possible.
  • This is your time to share your achievements.


What if I do not understand the question?

  • Well before the day of your review, speak to your line manager and seek clarification.


What if I get a lot of ‘does not meet’ ratings?

  • As with all formal discussions, the sole outcome we all seek is that we want you to improve performance. This is the opportunity to be honest and seek help from your line manager.


Appraisal Ratings

  • Exceeds Requirements
    • Performance is consistently at a higher standard than would normally be expected for this position.
  • Meets Requirements
    • Performance is consistently of an appropriate standard for this position.
  • Opportunity for Development
    • Performance is at a lower than acceptable standard required for this position.
